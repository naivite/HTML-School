Dropkick Murphys

I was first introduced to the sounds of Dropkick Murphys, In late 2001, by a co-worker. I was stationed in Cheyenne, WY at the time, living in a dormatory. I had just entered the dormatory, when I heard the sounds of punk music, blaring down the hallway. As I made my way down the hallway, the sounds had ended and were replaced by the wailing sounds of bagpipes. These were new sounds to me, as I had never heard them played in the manner I was now hearing them played. The mixture was melodic and harsh, and I enjoyed it. I soon began prowling the halls, trying to find which room they had come from, to discover that it was coming from a co-workers room. The next day at work, I asked him who had been playing, and he invited me over to listen to what he had. Through them, I had learned of a whole other genre of music, and discovered Flogging Molly, The Pogues, Street Dogs, and The Ducky Boys. I am no longer in touch with the individual who introduced me to Dropkick Murphys, but I have not forgotten him.

As of today, Dropkick Murphys have released 9 studio albums, the most recent having been released January 6th, 2017. Out of their albums, The Gang's All Here is my favorite. I don't think it is because I love all the songs on it, which I do not, but because of where I was at the time. I had joined the military, in April of 2001. Our country was at a relative time of peace. I arrived to my first duty station, on September 3rd, 2001; and it was the first time I was on my own, in an unfamiliar place. Then 8 days later, our nation was devastated by the attacks in New York City and Washington DC, and as a service member I was now needed to defend our country. Months after the attacks is when I first heard this album. It let me forget about the grim feelings I had felt, and let me enjoy myself for the first time since arriving to Wyoming.
